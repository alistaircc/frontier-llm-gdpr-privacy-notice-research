Our role in your privacy


"We" are Vinted, UAB – the company behind the Vinted app and website, which you simply know as "Vinted". You'll find our contact details below.

When you use Vinted (and in some other cases we mention below), we act as your "data controller". That means we're responsible for looking after your data and making sure it's handled according to data protection laws like the General Data Protection Regulation ("GDPR").

To keep things transparent, we've put together key information on what data we collect and why, how we use it, who we share it with, how long we store it for, and how we keep it secure. We've also included your rights and choices when it comes to your data – so you're always in control.

Data we collect


From the moment you start interacting with Vinted, whether you're signing up, browsing the app, or getting in touch, you trust us with your data.

By "your data", we mean personal data. That's any information that can identify or be linked to you as an individual.

The categories and amount of data we collect depend on how you use Vinted and how you interact with us:

The data you provide
When you use Vinted or otherwise interact with us, you share different kinds of data:

Identification data, such as your full name.
Contact data, such as your email address, phone number, and address.
Account data, such as your username, password, verification details, and account settings.
Demographic data, such as your language, gender, and age (date of birth).
Location data, such as your country and city.
Activity data, such as your profile description, profile picture, holiday mode, Vinted members you follow, ratings and reviews, media uploads and similar information.
Listing data, such as details about the items you list, like the name, category, features, photos, price, and parcel size.
Communication data, such as messages you send and receive (including emails, in-app chats, and customer support conversations), plus the date, time, participants, and any attachments.
Financial data, such as your Vinted Balance, chosen payment method, payment card details (e.g. card identifier, last four digits), and bank account number.
Transactional data, such as purchase and payment history, donations, order records, invoices, receipts, and income reports.
Shipping data, such as the delivery method you choose, pick-up or drop-off addresses, and parcel tracking details.
Commercial data, if you're using Vinted as a Pro seller. This includes things such as your business name, registration numbers, and details about the business representative.
Feedback and survey data, including recordings from any interviews you may have taken part in.
Legal and compliance data, such as consent records, legal claims, copies of documents confirming that you own your Vinted account, and copies of your contracts with us.
We only collect the data that's needed based on what you do on Vinted. So if you haven't bought and sold anything, we won't have your financial, transactional, and shipping data. And if you haven't communicated with anyone, we won't have your communication data.

Most of the time, sharing your data is totally up to you. But in some cases, it's required by law or needed to enter into or perform a contract with you. Learn more about these situations and what happens if you don't provide your data.

The data we collect automatically
Some data is collected automatically when you use Vinted:

Activity data, such as the date and time of your session and how long it lasts, pages visited, search history, user journey, features used, as well as how you interact with our ads, or ads you've seen on other platforms.
Location data, such as your approximate location based on your IP address.
Device and technical data, such as your IP address and browser type, as well as the type of device you're using, your operating system, screen resolution, device settings, crash logs and technical diagnostics, and any unique device identifiers.
Security data, such as fraud or malware detection alerts, account restriction (blocking) details (dates, reasons), cybersecurity incident reports and similar information.
Data from other sources
We also receive some of your data from other sources:

Data from other platforms. If you sign up, log in, or verify your Vinted account with Google, Facebook, or Apple, we receive your full name, email address, and profile picture – depending on what you've allowed those platforms to share.
Data from service providers and partners. For example, when you use payment services on Vinted, our payment service providers share your transaction data (such as the date, status, or any issues), and security data (such as fraud alerts or restrictions). If you use our integrated shipping options, shipping providers share parcel tracking updates so we can keep you informed and help resolve any issues.
Data from other Vinted members. This might include reviews and ratings about you, as well as any issues reported about you by other members.
Data from social media. If you interact with Vinted content on social media, we may see your comments, messages, likes or other engagement.
Data from public sources. For example, we may see your public reviews of Vinted on third-party sites.
Data from other sources. We might also receive information about you from your representatives (for example, when they exercise your rights on your behalf), official authorities (like when they forward a request or complaint), rights holders (such as an intellectual property owner reporting a possible rights issue), or from companies we acquire or merge with.
